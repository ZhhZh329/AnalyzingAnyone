In 2016, Musk co-founded Neuralink with an investment of $100 million. The company aims to develop brain-machine interfaces. Musk has framed the mission in terms of AI existential risk: "If you can't beat them, join them. The merge with AI is the best-case scenario." In January 2024, Neuralink implanted its first brain chip in a human patient, who was subsequently able to control a computer cursor with his thoughts.
Source: Various, Neuralink presentations
