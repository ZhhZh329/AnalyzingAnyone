The Wall Street Journal reported that after Musk insisted on branding Tesla's driver-assistance features as "Autopilot" and "Full Self-Driving," he faced pushback from engineers who warned he was putting customer "lives at risk." Several engineers reportedly resigned in 2017 over the naming controversy. Despite this, Musk has repeatedly made bold predictions about fully autonomous driving that have not materialized on the timelines he stated. In 2016 he predicted fully autonomous cross-country drives by 2017. As of 2026, the technology still requires driver supervision.
Source: Wall Street Journal, various Tesla communications
