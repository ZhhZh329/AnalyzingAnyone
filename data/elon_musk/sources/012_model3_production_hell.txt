During the Model 3 production ramp in mid-2018, Musk moved into the Fremont factory, sleeping on the production floor for months. In interviews he stated: "If the workers are suffering, I want to suffer with them. My suffering should be greater than theirs."

He personally intervened in production line design, often overriding his engineering team's recommendations. At one point he ordered the removal of an expensive automated conveyor system that had been installed just months earlier, replacing it with manual labor. His explanation: "Excessive automation at Tesla was a mistake. Humans are underrated."

During this period he regularly worked 120-hour weeks. He later described it as "the most painful year of my career — production hell." Tesla came within single-digit weeks of bankruptcy before achieving its 5,000 cars/week production target in late June 2018.
Source: Multiple interviews, Walter Isaacson biography
