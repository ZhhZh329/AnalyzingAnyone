SpaceX's first three Falcon 1 rocket launches failed. The first (March 2006) failed due to a corroded fuel line nut. The second (March 2007) reached space but the second stage spun out of control. The third (August 2008) failed when the first and second stages collided during separation. After the third failure, Musk reportedly had just enough money for one more attempt. A colleague recalled: "I'd never seen Elon look that defeated. He said we would keep going until we ran out of money." The fourth launch, on September 28, 2008, succeeded — making Falcon 1 the first privately developed liquid-fuel rocket to reach orbit.
