In various interviews, Musk has described entrepreneurship in visceral terms: "Starting a company is like staring into the abyss and eating glass." When asked by an investor about his commitment to SpaceX's early days, he reportedly said: "I'd rather commit seppuku than fail." A recurring theme in interviews is his willingness to take personal financial risk: "I could either watch my companies die or put all my remaining money into them. That's not really a choice. I don't ever give up. I'd have to be dead or completely incapacitated."
Source: Various interviews
