In 2013, Musk published a 57-page white paper proposing the Hyperloop — a high-speed transportation system capable of moving passengers at nearly 800 mph. He described it as "a fifth mode of transportation" and open-sourced the concept rather than building it himself, saying he was too busy with SpaceX and Tesla. He later said: "I just wanted to show it was possible." Critics noted this was a recurring pattern — proposing revolutionary ideas and leaving execution to others, which some saw as visionary and others as irresponsible distraction.
Source: Hyperloop Alpha white paper (2013), various interviews
