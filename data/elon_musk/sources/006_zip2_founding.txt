In 1995, Musk co-founded Zip2 with his brother Kimbal. They worked out of a small rented office in Palo Alto. Musk coded the website every night, often sleeping at the office. The company developed an internet city guide for newspapers. Musk's immigration status during this period was later described as a "gray area" — a Washington Post investigation in 2024 reported he may have been working without proper authorization. In 1999, Compaq acquired Zip2 for $307 million. Musk received $22 million from the sale.
Source: Washington Post (2024), Ashlee Vance biography
