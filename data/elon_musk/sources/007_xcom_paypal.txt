With his Zip2 proceeds, Musk founded X.com in 1999, an online financial services company. X.com merged with Confinity (which had a product called PayPal) in 2000. There was a bitter power struggle over the company's direction. In September 2000, while Musk was on his honeymoon, the board ousted him as CEO and replaced him with Peter Thiel. Musk later said: "That's the problem with vacations." Despite losing the CEO role, Musk remained the largest shareholder. When eBay acquired PayPal for $1.5 billion in 2002, Musk received $176 million.
Source: Various, including "The PayPal Wars" by Eric M. Jackson
