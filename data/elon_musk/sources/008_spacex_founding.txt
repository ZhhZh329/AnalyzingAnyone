In 2001, Musk traveled to Russia to buy refurbished ICBMs for launching payloads to Mars. The Russians reportedly didn't take him seriously and quoted exorbitant prices. On the flight home, Musk opened a spreadsheet and calculated the raw material costs of building a rocket from scratch. He concluded he could build one for about 10% of the going rate. He founded SpaceX in 2002 with $100 million of his own money. He has said: "I thought the chance of success was less than 10%. I just accepted that I would probably lose everything."
Source: Ashlee Vance biography, multiple interviews
