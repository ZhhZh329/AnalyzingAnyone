At age 10, Musk got a Commodore VIC-20 and taught himself to code using a BASIC manual. At 12, he created a video game called Blastar and sold it to a South African magazine for approximately $500. On an IBM aptitude test for coding at age 14, his score was so high it reportedly couldn't be properly assessed by the standard rubric.
Source: Ashlee Vance biography (2015)
