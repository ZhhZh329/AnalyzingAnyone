At the 2016 International Astronautical Congress in Guadalajara, Mexico, Musk presented his plan to colonize Mars:

"History is going to bifurcate along two directions. One path is we stay on Earth forever, and then there will be some eventual extinction event. The alternative is to become a space-faring civilization and a multi-planetary species."

On the economics: "The cost of moving to Mars needs to be roughly equivalent to the median house price in the US — around $200,000."

When asked about the risk of death: "The first journeys to Mars will be very dangerous. The risk of fatality is quite high. There's no way around it. Are you prepared to die? If that's okay, then you're a candidate for going."

He has also said: "I would like to die on Mars. Just not on impact."
Source: IAC 2016 presentation
