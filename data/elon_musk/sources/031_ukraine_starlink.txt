In February 2022, days after Russia invaded Ukraine, Musk sent Starlink satellite terminals to Ukraine after a direct request from a Ukrainian government official on Twitter. This provided critical internet access to the Ukrainian military and civilians. However, Musk later faced criticism when it emerged that he had secretly restricted Starlink's range near Crimea, preventing its use in a Ukrainian attack on Russian naval vessels. Musk explained he feared enabling a potential nuclear escalation. He said: "If I had agreed to their request, SpaceX would be explicitly complicit in a major act of war."
Source: Walter Isaacson biography (2023), various news reports
